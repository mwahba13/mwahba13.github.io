### Egregore
**Role:** Creative Director
**Duration:** 2023 – 2024
**Engine:** Unreal Engine 5
**Tags:** Game Direction, Unreal Engine 5, C++, Blueprints, Narrative Design, Systems Design

USC MFA thesis game — a first-person adventure mystery set in modern-day Cairo. Inspired by classic adventure games like Myst, it innovates on the genre with a novel **graph-based perception mechanic** that lets players reshape their understanding of the world by changing how they perceive information.

**Role & Responsibilities:**
- Narrative design, worldbuilding, and dialogue systems
- Game mechanics and systems design — including the core graph-based perception mechanic
- Level design and environmental storytelling
- Team management and production coordination across a 20-person development team

**Highlights:**
- Led a 20+ person team across design, art, engineering, and audio
- Original soundtrack composed by USC Thornton School of Music students
- Selected for USC's prestigious Advanced Games Program
- Featured at the 2024 Game Devs of Color Expo and USC Games Expo

### The Immersive Archive
**Role:** XR Technical Lead
**Duration:** 2023 – 2024
**Award:** AWE 2024 Best in Show
**Tags:** XR Research, Unity, OpenXR, XR Development

A research and development project dedicated to recreating and preserving seminal XR experiences from the early days of the medium. Started at the **Mobile and Environmental Media Lab at USC**, the project combines faithful recreation with scholarly documentation to build a living record of XR history — ensuring landmark XR works that no longer run on modern hardware remain accessible to researchers, educators, and future creators.

**Responsibilities:**
- Defining the software architecture and technical roadmap for the project
- Prototyping and iterating multiple modules recreating seminal XR experiences in Unity
- Representing the project at SIGGRAPH 2023 and AWE 2024 (won Best in Show)
- Co-authoring a paper published in IEEE Conference Proceedings

**Links:** [Project Website](https://immersivearchive.org/) | [Video Playthrough](https://youtu.be/iyYHyenP1YM?si=kDJJwbGOBoF9bu5z) | [IEEE Conference Proceedings](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10896116) | [SIGGRAPH 2023 Panel](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3587422.3595554#)

### Atrio: The Dark Wild
**Role:** Lead Writer
**Duration:** 2023 – 2024
**Platform:** PC (Steam)
**Tags:** Narrative Design, Dialogue Writing, Worldbuilding, Character Development

A commercially released survival-automation game that blends base-building and resource management with a rich, character-driven narrative. Set in a world where light is a scarce and precious resource, players automate systems to survive while uncovering the deeper truths of their world.

**Responsibilities:**
- Creating the game's overall narrative arc, lore, and thematic framework

**Narrative Design Highlights:**
- Environmental storytelling through world design and discoverable lore
- Integrated narrative progression tied to automation and survival mechanics
- Character relationships that evolve dynamically based on player choices and progression
