Unreal Engine 5 Blueprint C++

Egregore is a first-person adventure game taking place in modern day Cairo and is my MFA thesis completed at the University of Southern California in 2024.

Inspired by classic adventure games like Myst, it innovates on the genre by introducing a novel graph-based mechanic where players can alter the world by changing their perception of information. I created this game to let players explore the vibrant streets of Contemporary Cairo in ways never before possible.

Highlights
  • Original concept, prototypes and pitch decks
  • Led development team of 30+ students
  • Original soundtrack via USC Thornton School of Music
  • Selected for USC "Advanced Games Program"
  • Featured in 2024 Game Devs of Color Expo and USC Games Expo

Unity C# AR/VR

The Immersive Archive is an attempt to recreate and preserve seminal XR experiences from the earlier days of the medium. The same way that a film archive would preserve classic films, the Immersive Archive aims to preserve and make accessible classic XR experiences that have been lost to time.

Started in the Mobile and Environmental Media Lab at USC, this project is focused on ensuring the history of XR remains accessible to future generations.

Highlights
  • Responsible for the software architecture and technical roadmap
  • Prototyped and iterated several modules recreating seminal XR experiences
  • Shown at SIGGRAPH 2023 and AWE 2024 (Best in Show Award)

Radio Exurbia
Media Gallery
Unreal Engine C++ Blueprints

Radio Exurbia is a driving adventure game where the player explores alternate realities by switching radio stations. This game was created by me and one of my classmates, Jimi Stine, as a semester-long project at USC.

We were inspired by games like Exo One and Art of Rally that are more about moving through a space with expression than posting a high score.

Highlights
  • Driving adventure game with a reality-switching radio
  • A drivable playground for the player to explore
  • Features an original soundtrack via Berklee School of Music

L is a prototype that I find myself constantly revisiting. I am fascinated by the idea of Lindenmayer systems and how they could be used as a game mechanic.

The same way that Case of the Golden Idol gives players a grammar to solve mysteries, I wanted to give players a grammar to generate beautiful structures driven by an underlying mathematical logic reflective of nature. Although this prototype is bare, it is an idea I continue to explore...

Javascript/WebGL Gaussian Splatting AR/VR

The Virtual Garden takes advantage of the emerging technology of Gaussian Splatting to create an immersive narrative taking the viewer through photorealistic environments. This project was created as part of an independent research project at the Mobile and Environmental Media Lab at USC.

In November 2024, we presented our work and the tools we developed to create this experience in a talk "Gaussian Splatting for Immersive Storytelling" at DevFestYYC in Calgary, Alberta.

Highlights
  • Leveraged LumaAI's WebGL library to make a toolchain for stringing scenes together.
  • Presented work at a workshop at DevFestYYC 2024
